Features

Every feature is in service of clinical judgment.

Psygil is opinionated. It makes the right thing easy and the wrong thing impossible. AI organizes evidence. The clinician makes every decision the referral, the court, or the payer cares about. Built for forensic work. Rigorous enough for clinical evaluation.

The gate

Decision gates stop the AI from diagnosing

The Diagnostician agent reads everything the clinician has gathered and proposes an evidence map. For every diagnosis it considers relevant, it shows the DSM-5-TR criteria, the ICD-10 code, and the supporting quotes pulled from the case file. The agent does not diagnose.

The clinician renders, defers, or rejects. The clinician can add diagnoses the agent missed. The clinician can override on every line. Reasoning is captured in a clinical notes field, timestamped, signed, and written to the audit log.

The Writer agent refuses to run until at least one diagnosis has been rendered. No skip. No AI-only path. The doctor diagnoses, or the report does not exist.

The pipeline, stage by stage
01 Intake

The case begins where a real evaluation begins

A structured intake opens the moment a new case is created. Identity, insurance, referral source, referral question, charges or claim, legal history, family history, medical history, substance use, presenting complaints. The clinician fills it in, not the AI. Every field is typed, every entry is timestamped.

02 Testing

The clinician picks the tests. The clinician scores the tests

Psygil tracks every instrument administered, who scored it, when, and the result. For forensic, that might mean ECST-R and MacCAT-CA. For clinical, PAI, MMPI-2-RF, BDI-II, WAIS-IV. The Ingestor agent summarizes prior records before testing begins. From there, the decisions are the clinician's.

03 Interviews

Psygil does not sit in. Psygil keeps the record

Psygil does not record audio. The clinician meets the examinee face to face, then enters structured mental status observations, behavioral notes, the examinee's account, and responses to competency, insanity, or clinical questioning. Every quote is tied to the date and context it was recorded.

04 Reports

Writer drafts. Editor checks. Clinician signs

With the clinical decisions made, the Writer agent drafts the report. It pulls from the clinician's intake, interview notes, test results, and only the diagnoses the clinician has personally rendered. The Editor reviews for internal consistency and citation accuracy. The clinician attests. The report is locked.

Privacy and PII

Names never leave the laptop

Psygil runs Microsoft Presidio and a spaCy NER model locally in a sidecar process. PII detection, redaction, and rehydration all happen on the clinician's machine. Names, addresses, phone numbers, and identifiers are never sent to a remote service for detection.

When the AI agents run against remote model providers, Psygil redacts first and rehydrates on the clinician's side. The upstream model sees tokens, not patients.

Discoverable record

Every decision, reproducible on demand

Every field entered, every test scored, every record ingested, every agent run, every diagnosis rendered, every draft revision, and every signature is written to an append-only audit log.

When a case goes to court, or a payer audits a clinical evaluation, the clinician exports a package that includes the intake, test raw scores, interview notes, source documents, agent outputs, clinical decisions with rationale, report revisions, and cryptographic hashes for every file. Cross-examination becomes a matter of reading the record, not reconstructing it.
